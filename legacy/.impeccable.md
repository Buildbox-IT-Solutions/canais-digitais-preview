## Design Context

### Users
B2B professionals across 11+ industry verticals (food & beverage, health, construction, architecture, etc.). They access Informa Markets editorial portals during work hours, typically on desktop. The dashboard is their logged-in account hub — managing profile data, downloads, newsletters, and security. They visit infrequently and want to accomplish tasks quickly without friction.

### Brand Personality
**Neutral, institutional, scalable.** The design system must work across very different verticals without favoring any. Personality comes from content and categorical colors, not from the structure itself. The logged-in area maintains the same institutional tone as the public portal — no warmer, no more playful.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone:** Clean, serious, strong typographic hierarchy. Editorial DNA even in utilitarian screens.
- **Typography:** Aleo (serif, display/headline/title) + Open Sans (body/label). MD3 17-scale system.
- **Palette:** Primary deep navy (#002244), secondary sky blue (#28B4FF), neutral slate. Categorical colors (mint, coral, saffron, lavender) for editorial differentiation.
- **Theme:** Light mode. Dark mode planned but not in scope.
- **Anti-references:** LinkedIn settings (cluttered, noisy, too many competing actions). Generic SaaS dashboards with stat cards everywhere.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG AA.

### Design Principles
1. **Institutional neutrality** — the structure never competes with content. No decorative flourishes.
2. **Typographic hierarchy first** — Aleo headlines create rhythm; Open Sans carries information. Size and weight do the work, not color or decoration.
3. **One task at a time** — each section has a single purpose. Progressive disclosure over information overload.
4. **Token fidelity** — every value comes from the design system. No arbitrary colors, spacing, or radii.
5. **Scalable patterns** — components must work identically across 11+ portals. No portal-specific hacks.
